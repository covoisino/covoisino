## 2. Auth-driven routing

Right now your `initialRoute` is hard-coded to `'/signup'`. As soon as the user re-opens the app, they always see the signup screen—regardless of whether they’re already signed in. You might consider instead:

* Listening to `FirebaseAuth.instance.authStateChanges()` (or using `StreamBuilder`) at the top of your widget tree to choose between signup/login vs. home.
* Or, in `main()`, checking `FirebaseAuth.instance.currentUser` and setting your initial route accordingly (e.g. `/home` if non-null).

## 4. Controller disposal

You dispose your controllers in  **SetupPage** , but **not** in:

* `SignupPageState` (_firstNameController, _lastNameController, etc.)
* `LoginPageState` (_emailController, _passwordController)
* `ForgotPasswordPageState` (_emailController)
* …and a few others.

Always override `dispose()` and call `_xxxController.dispose()`, or you’ll leak memory every time those pages are pushed/popped.

## 7. Localization

* You roll your own `SimpleLocalizations`, which is fine for a toy app, but:
  * You’ll need to add arb/intl support (or keep this in sync manually) if you ever localize more than a handful of strings.
  * Your `onGenerateTitle` uses `SimpleLocalizations.of(ctx)` but your AppBar titles still have hard-coded English (e.g. `AppBar(title: Text('Signup'))`). You’ll want to replace those with `L.get('signup')` (and add that key).

## 8. UX flow & edge cases

* **Email verification restart**

  In `VerifyAccountPage._restart()` you do `user!.delete()`. If that throws (e.g. recently logged in via social provider without a password), your user might get stuck. Consider wrapping in `try/catch` and forcing a sign-out instead.
* **Stage skipping**

  If the user taps “Skip” on phone verification (stage 1 → stage 3), you never write anything to Firestore or prefs about that choice. That’s fine, but make sure your analytics/metrics can distinguish “skipped” vs. “entered invalid phone.”
* **Date‐of‐birth format**

  You store the DOB as a string `"yyyy-mm-dd"`. If you ever need to query ranges in Firestore, you’ll wish it was a true `Timestamp`.

### Summary

Nothing is “broken” per se, but before you ship:

1. Wire up your generated `firebase_options.dart` and guard for web.
2. Drive your initial screen from `authStateChanges()` instead of always going to signup.
3. Dispose **all** your TextEditingControllers.
4. Fill in your phone‐auth callbacks (and handle errors).
5. Commit your Firestore batches only when there’s data, and catch errors.
6. Sign out of *every* provider on logout (Google, Facebook, Apple).
7. Finish wiring localization everywhere.
8. Double‐check edge‐cases in your setup flow (deletion, skips, date format).

With those addressed, you’ll have a rock-solid foundation!
